Desert Island Disks

The five songs I can’t live without.

‘Downtown’ - Majical Cloudz

I would be hard pressed to find a song that means quite as much to me as this one. ‘Downtown’ is by far my most listened to track, racking up over two thousand listens since I discovered it just under ten years ago. I was first introduced to the song through one of my favourite shows, Brit Marling’s The OA (2016), which I remember vividly watching over and over in the summer following the end of my GCSE exams. The scene in which ‘Downtown’ features is one which requires a whole red-string board of explanation, so I won’t try to unravel that here.

‘Downtown’ is a melancholy, hypnotic ballad which immediately sunk its claws into me and has refused to budge ever since. It has a rare power that I have not found in any other songs: it has grown with me, capturing my sense of being both at sixteen and twenty-five. This is the song that I show to people when I want them to know me. It is both love and heartbreak, comfort and loneliness.

‘With or Without You’ - U2

I don’t remember the first time I heard U2’s iconic single ‘With Or Without You’. I was probably so young that it passed unnoticed into the background of my life, carrying with it a steady stream of memories into my adult life. Bono is a juggernaut in this song, it’s one of U2’s biggest hits for a reason. The only way I can describe his voice is transcendent. If I listen to this song alone at night I can feel almost as if I’ve cheated death. It’s a beautiful and painful depiction of a troubled relationship. And the melody is just so damn good.

‘My Kind of Woman’ - Mac DeMarco

This is by far the newest addition to the list. I discovered this song only at the start of 2026, a few days before New Year. In the span of a few days of travelling to Norway, I had managed to rack up over 600 listens (it helped that I had the 14-hour journey from hell there and back again). The first chord lassoed me with the grip of a noose and every time I listen it never loses its shine. It is a difficult decision to rate a song so high when I have only known it for half a year, but you know what they say: when you know, you know.

One thing I love about ‘My Kind of Woman’ is the artful panning (stereophonic sound playing different melodies and rhythms in each headphone). I can almost always be found with my trusty (for the eight-month shelf life) wired Apple headphones, and one of my favourite things to do while listening into this song is to focus in turn on the layered elements in each respective headphone. It’s a technique I’ve loved for a while, one which I first grew to adore in a near-inclusion in this list, ‘The Masterplan’ by Oasis. DeMarco somehow manages to capture just about every emotion of the last six months in this one song, from falling in love to falling apart. Even if in years to come this song loses some of its original excitement, it will still doubtlessly bring me right back here, to these months of my life.

‘Read My Mind’ - The Killers

It’s a big ask to name my favourite song by The Killers. I’ve been a fan since I was probably fourteen or fifteen, cemented completely in my years at sixth form college, where I have many a treasured memory of listening to their entire discography between college, home and work. However, it only takes a little relaxation of the brain for the answer to become achingly clear. It’s got to be ‘Read My Mind’, a standout single from their second album, Sam’s Town. While at a push, I would probably choose Hot Fuss for a desert island album, ‘Read My Mind’ stands out to be as one of the greatest Killers songs and one which has the most emotional pull in my life.

It’s the bridge that really captures me. I remember being obsessed with it while writing my first full-length novel (which has since been shelved and will likely never see the light of day). I love the music video, which features the band members roving around Tokyo. I love the instrumentals, I love the lyrics (‘slippin’ in my faith until I fall’ is simply brilliant), I love the beautiful sense of scale and the Springsteen-esque vibe. It’s simply a classic to me.

‘No 1 Party Anthem’ - Arctic Monkeys

Although these aren’t ranked in order, I think it’s fitting to round off this list with Arctic Monkeys’ ‘No 1 Party Anthem’. It’s the last song at the party, it’s collecting the rubbish at the end of the night and wondering where it all went wrong. This song could make me cry. It’s haunting in more ways than one, and there are years of my life that are lost within its hypnotic melody. It’s not a love song, rather far from it, which is once again fitting for the period of my life I associate it with.

Again, the bridge seals the deal. It is the feeling of looking across the room at someone and knowing you have to let them go. In my opinion, it’s AM at their finest, no notes. I’ve spent many an afternoon perfecting a music video for this song in my mind, daydreaming about an opportunity to direct it for myself one day. Increasingly it looks unlikely, but that doesn’t stop me entertaining it. It’s a good way to round off this list, the moment it ends you think ‘yeah, time to head home.’
